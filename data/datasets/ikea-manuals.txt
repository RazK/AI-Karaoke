KALLAX SHELF UNIT — ASSEMBLY INSTRUCTIONS
Article Number: 403.518.82
Read all instructions before beginning assembly.
Two people recommended for this step. Keep all small parts away from children under 3 years.

PARTS LIST
Panel A (top/bottom) x2
Panel B (side) x2
Panel C (divider, vertical) x3
Panel D (divider, horizontal) x3
Back panel E x1
Cam lock nut x24
Cam lock bolt x24
Dowel pin x32
Screw, 5x50mm x16
Screw, 3.5x15mm x8
Allen key, 4mm x1 (supplied)

Step 1 of 18
Lay panel A flat on a clean, padded surface. Ensure the pre-drilled holes face upward. Insert four dowel pins into the marked holes along the long edge. Press each dowel firmly until flush with the surface.

Step 2 of 18
Align panel B (side panel) with panel A. Match the dowel holes on panel B with the protruding dowels on panel A. Press panels together firmly. You should hear a click indicating proper alignment.

Step 3 of 18
Insert cam lock bolts into the pre-drilled holes on panel B. Turn each bolt counterclockwise until the head is flush. Do not overtighten at this stage.

Step 4 of 18
Insert cam lock nuts into the recessed openings on panel A. Use the supplied Allen key to rotate each nut clockwise until it engages with the cam lock bolt. Tighten to approximately 3 Nm. Do not overtighten or you may damage the cam mechanism.

Step 5 of 18
Repeat steps 1 through 4 for the opposite panel A and panel B combination. Ensure all parts are included before beginning this step.

Step 6 of 18
Position vertical divider panel C between the two assembled side panels. Align the pre-drilled holes with the corresponding holes in the top and bottom panels. Insert dowel pins and cam lock bolts as described in steps 1 through 4. Tighten all fasteners securely.

Step 7 of 18
Attach the remaining vertical dividers. Ensure each divider is perfectly vertical before tightening fasteners. Use a spirit level if available.

Step 8 of 18
Slide horizontal divider panel D into the recessed runners on the vertical dividers. Press firmly until the panel clicks into the locked position. Check alignment from the front.

WARNING: Do not stand or sit on any shelf during assembly. Maximum load per shelf: 13 kg. Exceeding this limit may cause structural failure.

Step 9 of 18
Turn the assembled frame face down onto a clean, padded surface. Position back panel E so all edges align with the frame. Secure using 3.5x15mm screws at every marked position. Drive screws into the pre-drilled holes using a screwdriver. Do not use a power drill for this step — overtightening will strip the holes.

Step 10 of 18
Turn the unit upright. Verify that the back panel is flush and the unit stands without wobbling. If the unit wobbles, loosen the corner fasteners, reposition, and retighten.

HEMNES DRESSER — ASSEMBLY INSTRUCTIONS
Article Number: 702.426.13
Read all instructions before beginning assembly.
Two people recommended. Total assembly time: approximately 60 minutes.

PARTS LIST
Side panel x2
Top panel x1
Bottom panel x1
Drawer front x3
Drawer box x3
Drawer runner, left x3
Drawer runner, right x3
Back panel x1
Handle x6
Wooden knob x0
Hinge x0
Rail, bottom x2
Peg, plastic x8
Screw, 4x40mm x20
Screw, 4x16mm x12
Bolt, M6x40 x8
Washer, 6mm x8
Allen key, 5mm x1

Step 1 of 22
Lay both side panels parallel on a clean floor. Orient them so the pre-drilled holes face each other (inward). Insert plastic pegs into the lower holes along the inner face of each side panel.

Step 2 of 22
Position the bottom panel between the side panels, resting it on the plastic pegs. Align the bolt holes in the side panels with those in the bottom panel.

Step 3 of 22
Insert M6x40 bolts through the side panel holes and into the bottom panel. Place a 6mm washer onto each bolt before inserting. Hand-tighten only at this stage.

Step 4 of 22
Attach the top panel using the same method. Ensure the front edge of the top panel is flush with the front edge of the side panels. Insert bolts and washers, hand-tighten only.

Step 5 of 22
Check that all four corners are square. Measure diagonally from corner to corner — both measurements should be equal. If not, gently push or pull the frame to square it, then tighten all bolts with the Allen key. Tighten to 5 Nm.

Step 6 of 22
Attach the lower rail to the bottom panel. Align the pre-drilled holes and secure using 4x16mm screws. Tighten fully.

Step 7 of 22
Mount the drawer runners. Align the left runner on the left side panel with the marked guide line. Secure using 4x16mm screws into the pre-drilled holes. Repeat for the right runner. Ensure both runners are level — use a spirit level if available.

Step 8 of 22
Repeat step 7 for each set of drawer runners. There are three drawer levels, each with left and right runners.

Step 9 of 22
Slide the first drawer box into the lowest pair of runners. Push until the runner mechanism engages with a click. Pull the drawer out halfway and check that it slides smoothly without tilting. If the drawer tilts, adjust the runner height using the adjustment screw at the front of the runner.

Step 10 of 22
Attach the drawer front to the drawer box. Hold the front in position so it is flush with the frame. Mark the position of the mounting holes through the predrilled holes in the drawer box onto the inside of the drawer front. Insert 4x40mm screws from inside the drawer box into the drawer front. Do not overtighten — the front should be held firmly but not warped.

Step 11 of 22
Attach the drawer handles. Position each handle on the drawer front centered according to the measurement guide on the instruction sheet. Insert mounting bolts from inside the drawer through the handle holes. Tighten fully with a screwdriver.

Step 12 of 22
Repeat steps 9 through 11 for the remaining drawers.

Step 13 of 22
Secure the back panel using 4x16mm screws into the pre-drilled holes around the perimeter of the back of the frame. Start from one corner and work outward. This panel is critical for structural rigidity — do not skip this step.

Step 14 of 22
Check that all drawers open and close smoothly. Each drawer should travel its full depth without binding. If a drawer binds, check that the runners are parallel and adjust as needed.

WARNING: Secure this furniture to the wall using the supplied wall mount and screw before use. Unsecured furniture can tip, which can crush and kill a child. Ensure wall fixings are appropriate for your wall type.

POÄNG CHAIR — ASSEMBLY INSTRUCTIONS
Article Number: 898.299.60
Two people recommended for final frame connection.

PARTS LIST
Bentwood frame x1
Seat cushion x1
Frame bolt, M8x60 x4
Washer, 8mm x4
Allen key, 6mm x1

Step 1 of 8
Unpack the bentwood frame. Inspect for damage before proceeding. The frame is laminated birch and may crack if forced.

Step 2 of 8
Position the seat cushion onto the frame. Align the rear tabs of the cushion with the hooks on the upper rear of the frame. Press down and forward until the cushion snaps into place.

Step 3 of 8
Connect the lower frame section to the upper section. Align the bolt holes. Insert four M8x60 bolts with washers. Hand-tighten only.

Step 4 of 8
Sit in the chair briefly to compress the cushion and settle the frame alignment. Then tighten all four bolts fully using the Allen key. Tighten in a cross pattern: front-left, rear-right, front-right, rear-left. Tighten to 6 Nm.

Step 5 of 8
Check that the chair sits level on the floor. Adjust by loosening and repositioning the lower frame if needed.

Step 6 of 8
Verify that all bolts are fully tightened before first use. Check bolts again after one week of use.

BESTA CABINET — ASSEMBLY INSTRUCTIONS
Article Number: 592.178.44

PARTS LIST
Side panel x2
Top/Bottom panel x2
Shelf x2
Door, hinged x2
Hinge x4
Door hinge cover x8
Shelf pin x8
Back panel x1
Leg, adjustable x4

Step 1 of 20
Insert shelf pins into the desired holes on the inner face of each side panel. Shelf pins allow you to position the shelf at multiple heights. Choose the height before assembly.

Step 2 of 20
Connect the side panels to the top and bottom panels using cam lock bolts and nuts. Follow the same cam lock procedure as described in the KALLAX instructions.

Step 3 of 20
Place the shelf onto the shelf pins. The shelf should rest level. Do not secure the shelf — it rests on the pins only and can be repositioned later.

Step 4 of 20
Mount the adjustable legs to the bottom panel using 4x16mm screws. Insert the leg stem into the mounting plate. Adjust leg height using a flathead screwdriver in the adjustment slot at the base of the leg. All four legs must be at equal height before placing the cabinet upright.

Step 5 of 20
Secure the back panel using staples or tacks (not supplied). The back panel holds the cabinet square. Ensure all edges align before fastening.

Step 6 of 20
Attach the hinges to the door panels. Position each hinge according to the measurement guide. Insert mounting screws into the pre-drilled hinge holes. Tighten fully.

Step 7 of 20
Mount the door to the cabinet frame. Hook the hinge arms onto the cabinet-side hinge plates. Press until the hinge clicks into place. Check that the door hangs parallel to the cabinet edge.

Step 8 of 20
Adjust the door alignment using the adjustment screws on the hinge. Turn the height adjustment screw to raise or lower the door. Turn the side adjustment screw to move the door left or right. Turn the depth adjustment screw to move the door forward or back.

Step 9 of 20
Attach the door hinge covers by pressing them onto each hinge until they click. These covers are decorative only.

Step 10 of 20
Attach handles or knobs to the doors using the supplied bolts. Position centered on the door stile (vertical edge strip) as shown in the diagram. Insert the bolt from inside the door through the hole and into the handle shank. Tighten fully.

GENERAL WARNINGS AND NOTES
Ensure all parts are included before beginning assembly. Missing parts should be reported to the IKEA store or ordered from the IKEA website.
Do not use impact drivers or power tools for cam lock fasteners.
Tighten all fasteners before applying any load to the furniture.
Check all fasteners after the first week of use and periodically thereafter.
Two people recommended for assembling units taller than 100cm.
Wall anchoring is required for all tall units and units placed in rooms accessible to children.
Dispose of packaging responsibly. Cardboard is recyclable. The plastic bag in which the hardware is supplied is recyclable at most grocery stores.

Total estimated assembly time: 45 to 90 minutes depending on experience and unit size.
Torque specifications: cam lock nuts 3 Nm, M6 bolts 5 Nm, M8 bolts 6 Nm.
Tool required: Allen key (supplied). Screwdriver (not supplied). Spirit level (not supplied, recommended).
Dimensions of assembled KALLAX unit: 77x147x39 cm (WxHxD).
Article number must be quoted when contacting customer service.
Step 1 of 24 begins at the hardware bag. Count all parts before starting.
